Title: Revolution of Our Times (2020)
Media: Oil Painting on Canvas
-
The series of protests that took place in Hong Kong, dubbed the Revolution of Our Times (時代革命), are a testament to the great sense of belonging that Hong-Kongers felt towards the city. Bearing construction hard hats, safety goggles, and industrial grade gas masks, millions took to the streets to protect the city's legacy. With tear gas and pepper spray heavy in the air – as depicted in the piece – they upkept an unfaltering stance and truly proved their loyalty to the city.

Title: Roots (2019)
Media: Wax Pastels and Colored Pencils on Wood
-
Inspired by Japanese-American artist Audrey Kawasaki, Roots is a piece that discusses complicated cultural and emotional ties. Bearing strong colour and floral symbolism, this piece uses negative space to depict a somewhat convoluded definition to heritage and belonging with blurred and soft lines that make up the peonies' outline. Its extended stems flowing about the figure loosely, varying in thickness and length, symbolise the everchanging stance we all have regarding identity.

Title: Home is Where the Heart is
Media: Digital Photography and Collaging, Screenprinting
-
With a figure that goes against the grain of what a typical Hong-Konger looks like, this piece – like many of my other pieces – explores the identity crisis and inner conflict that many have as they feel most connected to this unique and diverse city. Overlaid above Hong Kong landmarks, the figure remains the focal point as it is in bright red, symbolising the occurence of always standing out as a 'non-local' and the feeling of isolation.
Title: 舊式香港 (trans. Vintage Hong Kong)
Media: Digital Artwork and Photography
-
Inspired by the nightscape of Hong Kong, this piece pays homage to the iconic bright and saturated neon lights most prominent during the 1970's to 1990's. This artwork expresses the appreciation, to this day, that many Hong-Kongers have towards 'traditional' aspects of the city.
